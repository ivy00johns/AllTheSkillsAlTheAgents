# Safe Direction Examples

The "credible default" direction — recognizable, well-mannered, and unlikely to alienate the broadest audience. Use this slot when one of N directions needs to be the executive-ready version that any stakeholder can sign off on.

## When the Safe Direction Lives Here

A "safe" direction means: convention-respecting layout, restrained palette, neutral typography, predictable density. Specifically:

- **Layout:** single-column editorial or balanced split — nothing experimental
- **Typographic register:** neo-grotesk minimalism or display-serif gravity; never monospace dossier or oversized italic broadsheet
- **Image treatment:** half-bleed with metadata panel, or full-bleed cinematic; clean and recognizable
- **Color discipline:** cream + navy editorial, bone + neutral, or a single muted hue family
- **Density:** generous whitespace; the marketing-default register

The safe direction's job is to be the version the user can show their most conservative stakeholder without flinching. It still has to be opinion-dense — "safe" does not mean "generic SaaS."

## Worked Sample — Sovereign Sampson "Navy Field"

```text
Direction A: Navy Field
Palette: cream (#F5EFE6) + navy (#0F2747) + signal red accent (#B22222)
Display: GT Sectra (fallback: Source Serif 4 600)
Body: Söhne (fallback: Inter 400)
Mono: not used in this direction
Layout: single-column editorial, hero photo half-bleed top, copy reflows around metadata caption
Hero: half-bleed photo with mono caption block underneath
Density: generous whitespace, NYT-Mag register
Live elements: none — static editorial pages only
CTAs: subtle text-link "support" in nav; no sticky donate
Photos: real photos, placeholder frames in the canvas with caption captions
Tone reference apps: NYT Magazine + Politico Magazine
```

## What Makes the Safe Direction Distinct From Bold / Experimental

The variation rubric requires direction-to-direction differences on at least three orthogonal axes. The safe direction's "axis positions":

- **Layout axis:** editorial column (the most familiar pattern)
- **Register axis:** serif gravity (recognizable as "considered editorial work")
- **Treatment axis:** half-bleed photo with caption (familiar magazine pattern)
- **Color axis:** cream + navy (the most recognizable editorial palette in English-language print)

When you write the safe direction, pick the recognizable position on every axis. Save the unconventional moves for the bold and experimental slots — see `references/direction-examples/bold.md` and `references/direction-examples/experimental.md`.
